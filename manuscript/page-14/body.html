The Common Carpet Beetle.[3]

[3] Anthrenus scrophulariae L.

The common carpet beetle was known as the carpet beetle in the years following its introduction into America when floors were more commonly than, now covered entirely with carpets that were tacked down along the edges, thus giving the larvæ an undisturbed shelter favorable to their development. Originally a pest in Europe, where it is still common, it was introduced into this country about 1874, probably at Boston and New York simultaneously.
